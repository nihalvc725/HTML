Web designing and Publishing

Front end
Front-end development is the practice of building the visual and interactive elements of a website or application—the entire part that a user sees and directly interacts with in their browser. This process is focused entirely on the **User Experience (UX)**, ensuring the interface is responsive, accessible, fast, and visually appealing across all devices. The foundation of front-end development rests upon the three core languages: **HTML** for structuring the content, **CSS** for styling and visual layout, and **JavaScript** for dynamic behavior and interactivity, often enhanced by modern frameworks like React, Vue, or Angular.
Back end
Back-end development is the **server-side** work that manages an application's logic, data storage, and security. It handles everything the user doesn't see—processing requests, interacting with **databases**, and controlling **APIs**. Back-end engineers use languages like **Python**, **Java**, or **Node.js** to build the robust, high-performing foundation that powers the front-end.
